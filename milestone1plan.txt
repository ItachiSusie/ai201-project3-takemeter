MILESTONE 1 — COMMUNITY AND LABEL TAXONOMY
===========================================

Community: r/nba (Reddit NBA basketball discussion community)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
r/nba is one of Reddit's most active sports communities (~10M members), where
users debate player rankings, game performances, team decisions, and the
league's direction. The community self-polices discourse quality strongly —
members distinguish between posts that make genuine arguments and posts that
are emotional reactions or unsupported claims. This makes it an ideal community
for a discourse-quality classifier: the distinctions are real and culturally
meaningful inside the community.

Label Taxonomy: 3 Labels
-----------------------------------------------------------------

LABEL 1: analysis
Definition: A post that makes a structured argument backed by statistics,
historical comparisons, contextual data, or tactical/strategic reasoning.
Evidence is specific and verifiable; the post reasons toward a conclusion
rather than just asserting it.

Example 1:
"Luka's efficiency on pull-up threes this postseason (.412 3P%) puts him in
rare company — only Steph Curry and Dirk have posted higher rates in a single
playoff run with 5+ attempts per game. His step-back geometry is fundamentally
different: he releases at a higher apex, meaning defenders closing out get less
contest window. This isn't just hot — it's reproducible."

Example 2:
"People are sleeping on how much the Celtics' switch-everything scheme depends
on having a 7-footer who can guard guards. When Porzingis is off the floor,
their defensive rating drops from 107 to 119. The system doesn't work without
him — which is exactly why the Pacers targeted him with off-ball screens in Q4."

Borderline case for analysis:
"Kawhi's career playoff TS% is higher than LeBron's. That has to count for
something." — Cites a real stat but uses it to assert superiority without
deeper context. → Classified as HOT_TAKE (stat is cherry-picked for effect,
not as part of a reasoned argument. See decision rule below.)

---

LABEL 2: hot_take
Definition: A bold, confident opinion stated without supporting evidence, or
with evidence that is cherry-picked or decorative rather than genuinely
argumentative. The post asserts a strong claim; it does not build toward it.

Example 1:
"LeBron is the most overrated player in NBA history. His ring count only
happened because he had elite help every single time. Jordan would have 10
rings in LeBron's era."

Example 2:
"The NBA is soft now. Players take days off for 'load management' and nobody
calls it what it is — they're just scared of competing. The 90s players would
have laughed at this."

Borderline case for hot_take:
"Tatum is better than Luka right now — he's on a championship team and Luka
keeps losing in the first round." — Opinionated framing, points to a result as
implicit evidence. → Classified as HOT_TAKE (the evidence is a win/loss record
used rhetorically, not a reasoned performance argument).

---

LABEL 3: reaction
Definition: An immediate emotional response to a specific, recent game event,
play, or moment. Little or no argument — the post is expressing a feeling or
sharing a sentiment about something that just happened. Often short.

Example 1:
"THAT DUNK BY ANT OMFG HE JUST PUT THE WHOLE LEAGUE ON NOTICE"

Example 2:
"Can't believe we let that lead slip. This team breaks my heart every single
year. I'm done."

Borderline case for reaction:
"Steph just hit his 4th three of the half. At what point do teams stop leaving
him open? This is inexcusable coaching." — Reacts to a live event but adds a
light tactical critique. → Classified as REACTION (the comment is situational
and emotional; the 'critique' is not developed into an argument).

---

Edge Case Decision Rule: analysis vs. hot_take
------------------------------------------------
If a post provides specific, verifiable evidence that would support its claim
even if you stripped out the opinion framing → label it ANALYSIS.
If the evidence is vague, cherry-picked, or just enough to sound credible but
the post doesn't reason through it → label it HOT_TAKE.
When in doubt: if the post's main payload is the claim (not the reasoning),
it's a HOT_TAKE.

Edge Case Decision Rule: reaction vs. hot_take
------------------------------------------------
REACTION posts are anchored to a specific event that just happened (a game,
a play, a trade announced moments ago). HOT_TAKE posts make general claims
about players, teams, or the league that aren't tied to a single moment.
If you could post it in a vacuum without referencing a specific event → HOT_TAKE.
If it requires the context of "this just happened" to make sense → REACTION.

---
Community Summary (for planning.md):
r/nba is a text-heavy, opinion-rich community where discourse quality is
culturally meaningful — members actively distinguish analysis from noise.
The three-label taxonomy (analysis / hot_take / reaction) captures a
distinction that r/nba participants recognize and care about: whether a post
advances the conversation or merely expresses a feeling or a bold unsubstantiated
claim. These labels are mutually exclusive (grounded in evidence and framing
rather than topic) and cover at least 95% of subreddit posts cleanly.
